Hairless Dog Care

Xoloitzcuintli (Xolo)

Learn more about Xoloitzcuintlis at the American Kennel Club and Espree

Xolos require bathing as frequently as weekly to every 4 weeks. When bathing your Xolo, it is best practice to do a preliminary bath which will lift dirt, oil, and debris from the skin. It is also important to use the mildest shampoo possible. Younger dogs are prone to blackheads and acne and may need special care. Regular nail trimming is also required to keep your Xolo comfortable. Hairless dogs require sunscreen when exposed to the sun for longer periods of time.

Xolos are energetic dogs, liking long walks and upbeat play; however, they can be very calm when at home. Younger dogs required structured playtime and a lot of exercise to keep them happy and out of trouble.

Xolos should do well on any high-quality diet, but you should consult your veterinarian for guidance about your specific dog(s). Clean water should be available for your Xolo at all times.

American Hairless Terrier (AHT)

Learn more about American Hairless Terriers at the American Kennel Club and Wag

The American Hairless Terrier needs to be bathed at least once per week with a mild shampoo. If and when the AHT's skin becomes dry, a lanolin-free lotion will do the trick. Weekly nail trimmings and ear cleanings are required, and regular dental care will ensure that your AHT's teeth and gums remain healthy. AHTs also require sunscreen to prevent sunburn.

The American Hairless Terrier has moderate exercise needs, requiring several brief sessions in a yard or regular walks. AHTs enjoy activity but will also curl up on the couch with you at home.

AHTs should do well on any high-quality diet, but you should consult your veterinarian for guidance about your specific dog(s). Clean water should be available for your AHT at all times.

Peruvian Inca Orchid

Learn more about Peruvian Inca Orchids at the American Kennel Club and Wag

The Peruvian Inca Orchid is not an outdoor dog and requires sunscreen to protect them on sunny days. The occasional bath and regular nail trimming will keep your dog looking their best and prevent their nails from splitting, cracking, and overgrowing. Your Peruvian Inca Orchid's ears should be checked occasionally for wax buildup and debris and their teeth should be brushed when needed. A damp cloth can be used to remove dirt between baths, which should occur once per week or every two weeks. A regual dental routine is also required.

Peruvian Inca Orchids require a good bit of exercise to stay healthy. Regular walks and training for dog sports such as agility and obedience can offer your dog the exercise they need.

Peruvian Inca Orchids should do well on any high-quality diet, but you should consult your veterinarian for guidance about your specific dog(s). Clean water should be available for your Peruvian Inca Orchid at all times.

Chinese Crested

Learn more about Chinese Cresteds at the American Kennel Club and Espree

The hairless Chinese Crested is prone to skin irritation, allergies, and sunburn. Special care, careful grooming, sunscreen, and acne lotion can help maintain your dog's skin. Thorough baths are important, and a preliminary bath may help clear dirt, debris, and oil from your dog's skin. A mild shampoo is required for Chinese Crested dogs and baths should occur as frequently as weekly to every other week. A weekly brushing should keep your dog's coated areas from getting matted.

Chinese Crested dogs have lower exercise needs that can be satisfied with a play sessions in a yard or short walks.

Chinese Cresteds should do well on any high-quality diet formulated for toy breeds, but you should consult your veterinarian for guidance about your specific dog(s). Avoid giving table scraps, especially cooked bones and fatty foods. Clean water should be available for your Chinese Crested at all times.
