Health and Temperment

References: American Kennel Club, Wag, Espree

Xoloitzcuintli

Health

Xoloitzcuintlis have no known breed-specific health issues, but owners should look out for kennel cough, dry skin, teeth and gum issues, and sunburn.

Temperment

Xolos are gentle but sturdy enough to endure pets and hugs, and therefore great with children; however, small children should never be left with any dog unsupervised. Xolos are energetic, loyal, loving, and intelligent, sometimes being described as a "velcro dog". They are very easy to housebreak, but need a firm leader to discipline them They tend to bond to the person repsonsible for their food, general care, and training. They are naturally protective and shy around strangers.

American Hairless Terrier

Health

Major health concerns for American Hairless Terriers include patellar luxation, hemophilia, Legg-Calve-Perthes Disease, Cushing's Disease, teeth and gum issues, as well as liver shunts.

Temperment

American Hairless Terriers can be territorial and need a strong leader to keep them in line. They love to play and will make mischief on their own when left alone. Because they are poor swimmers, American Hairless Terriers needs to be watched closely around pools and other bodies of water. They also tend to sweat when too hot or when scared.

Peruvian Inca Orchid

Health

Major health concerns for Peruvian Inca Orchids include epilepsy, acne, skin lesions, and irritable bowel disease (IBS). Owners should also look out for teeth and gum issues.

Temperment

Peruvian Inca Orchids are laid-back dogs that prefer to be indoors. However, they can be lively and have a clown-like personality. It is best practice to socialize your Peruvian Inca Orchid from a young age so they can handle new situations with ease. They are sensitive, but easy to train, as long as respect and positive reinforcement are involved. It is recommended that they live in a home without small children. Peruvian Inca Orchids are shy around strangers, but can warm up to them over time.

Chinese Crested

Health

The Chinese Crested is generally a very healthy dog, but owners should look out for dentition and skin issues, as well as contact allergies.

Temperment

Chinese Cresteds are affectionate and enjoy the companionship of humans. They are often described as cat-like and can be found sitting in high places, like the back of the couch or the arm of a chair. They are active, but my hyper, and love children; however, supervision with children is a must. They get along with other dogs and don't bark very often. They are tough to housebreak, so patience is required when dealing with a Chinese Crested dog.
